My Story

I was an elite athlete. Then my body declared war on itself.

I grew up on a motocross bike. Pro-level racing — the kind that breaks bones and builds a different breed of human. Broken jaw. Shattered ribs — front and back. Broken scapula. Fluid-filled lung. Two knee surgeries. Back surgery. I lived at full throttle and accepted the cost.

But the real war wasn't on the track.

At 18 years old, I was diagnosed as clinically infertile. The doctors were definitive: "You will never father a child." That word — never — sat in my chest for years. But I refused to accept it as the final word.

"They said I would never have a child. In 2021, my daughter Grace was born — a miracle by every medical definition. Gerson Therapy, nutrition, and faithfulness in God reversed what medicine called irreversible."

Are you dealing with infertility and being told IVF is your only option?

I was told I had zero chance. No IVF. No intervention. Nutrition and faith reversed it. My daughter is alive because God doesn't operate within medical statistics.

At 22, I was diagnosed with degenerative disk disease. My MRI showed the spine of a 50-year-old. Doctors recommended pain management and eventual fusion surgery. I found Gerson Therapy — and my disks healed. Not managed. Healed. I'm 46 now — by their math, the back of a 76-year-old — and I'm still standing.

Then shingles — five times starting at 19. Then mono. Then Epstein-Barr. My immune system was screaming a pattern nobody was reading.

Lyme disease hit next. Then a traumatic brain injury that rewired my nervous system. Co-infections stacked: Cytomegalovirus, Babesia. The gut collapsed: SIBO, Candida, severe diverticulitis, an appendix infection resolved without surgery. Anemia, critically low B12. Hormones crashed: low testosterone, Hashimoto's, poor thyroid conversion, dangerously low cholesterol. Then POTS, adrenal fatigue, chronic fatigue syndrome.

Then: Carcinoma ex pleomorphic adenoma (CXPA) — a rare Stage III head and neck cancer. Two tumor removal surgeries. Fighting cancer while immunocompromised, neurologically impaired, hormonally depleted, and gut-compromised.

A 6mm indentation on my femur filled with severe arthritis. At 35, they said: "Full knee replacement within 2-3 years." I'm 46. Never had it. Never needed it.

I saw specialist after specialist. Every one brilliant in their lane. But no one was driving the car. I became my own strategist — not because I'm smarter than my doctors, but because I was the only one sitting in every appointment.
