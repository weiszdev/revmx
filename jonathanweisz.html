My Story

I was an elite athlete. Then my body declared war on itself.

I grew up on a motocross bike. Pro-level racing — the kind that breaks bones and builds a different breed of human. I lived at full throttle. My body was a machine.

But the machine started breaking. A broken jaw. Shattered ribs — front and back. A broken scapula. A fluid-filled lung. Two knee surgeries. A 6mm indentation on my femur filled with severe arthritis. At 35 years old, an orthopedic surgeon looked at the imaging and told me I'd need a full knee replacement. I'm 46 now. I've never had one. I still don't need one.

At 22, I was diagnosed with degenerative disk disease. The MRI showed a spine that looked like it belonged to a 50-year-old. Doctors offered pain management and eventually fusion surgery. Instead, I found Gerson Therapy — a nutrition-based regenerative protocol — and my disks healed. Not managed. Healed. I'm 46 now, which means by their math I have the back of a 76-year-old. But I'm still standing. Still moving. Still building.

"Nutrition changed my life. It gave me hope where 98 so-called experts had little to offer."

Then came shingles — not once, but five times, starting at age 19. Then mono. Then Epstein-Barr virus. My immune system was screaming, and nobody was listening to the pattern. They treated each outbreak like an isolated event. It wasn't.

The cascade continued: Lyme disease. A traumatic brain injury that rewired my nervous system. Co-infections stacking — Cytomegalovirus, Babesia. Then the gut collapsed: SIBO, Candida overgrowth, severe diverticulitis, an appendix infection that we resolved without surgery when every ER doctor said to cut. Iron-deficiency anemia, critically low B12 from malabsorption.

Hormones followed: low testosterone, poor thyroid conversion, Hashimoto's thyroiditis swinging between hyper and hypothyroid, dangerously low cholesterol that sounded healthy on paper but meant my body couldn't manufacture hormones. Then POTS, adrenal fatigue, chronic fatigue syndrome.

And then the diagnosis that changed everything: Carcinoma ex pleomorphic adenoma (CXPA) — a rare Stage III malignancy of the salivary gland. A cancer so uncommon most oncologists see it once or twice in a career. Two tumor removal surgeries. Fighting cancer while immunocompromised, neurologically impaired, hormonally depleted, and gut-compromised.

I saw specialist after specialist. Every one brilliant in their lane. But no one was driving the car. I realized that if I was going to survive, I needed to become my own strategist — not because I'm smarter than my doctors, but because I was the only one sitting in every appointment.

Gerson Therapy — How Nutrition Healed My Spine +

At 22, my MRI showed the spine of a 50-year-old. Degenerative disk disease. Multiple compressed and herniated disks. The recommendation was pain management, injections, and eventually fusion surgery.

I found Gerson Therapy — a nutrition-based protocol originally developed for cancer and degenerative disease. Fresh-pressed organic juices (13 per day in the full protocol), coffee enemas for liver detoxification, and a plant-based nutrient-dense diet designed to flood the body with the raw materials it needs to regenerate.

The result: my disks healed. Not managed. Not "stabilized." Healed. The same doctors who told me I'd be in a wheelchair by 40 have no explanation. I'm 46 and still building, still active, still racing motorcycles.

This is when I understood: the body was designed to heal. Our job is to remove what's blocking it and provide what it needs.

The Knee They Said I'd Replace at 35 +

A 6mm indentation on my femur, filled with severe arthritis. The orthopedic surgeon said: "You'll need a full knee replacement. Probably within the next 2-3 years." I was 35.

Instead of surgery, I went deep on anti-inflammatory nutrition, targeted supplementation (curcumin, collagen, omega-3), regenerative movement protocols, and strategic fasting to reduce systemic inflammation.

I'm 46 now. I have never had the knee replacement. I'm fully functional. The same knee that was "beyond repair" at 35 has carried me through 11 more years of life, fatherhood, and building businesses.

This isn't anti-medicine. This is pro-strategy. Sometimes the best intervention is giving the body what it needs instead of cutting out what's broken.

The Appendix Infection — Resolved Without Surgery +

Severe appendix infection. ER doctors were preparing for emergency surgery. The conventional answer was clear: cut it out.

Through aggressive IV antibiotics, anti-inflammatory protocols, and close monitoring, the infection resolved. The appendix was saved. No surgery. No recovery time. No scar.

This isn't always possible, and I'm not suggesting anyone refuse emergency surgery. But it taught me something critical: the first recommendation isn't always the only option. A strategist asks: "What else could we try first?"

Elite Athlete to Complex Case — The Full Arc +

Pro motocross. Full throttle. A body built for speed and impact. The injuries were part of the deal — broken jaw, shattered ribs (front and back), broken scapula, fluid-filled lung, two knee surgeries, back surgery. I accepted them as the cost of competition.

But the chronic illness wasn't from the track. Lyme came from a tick. TBI came from accumulated impact. The infections, the autoimmunity, the cancer — those came from a body under relentless stress with an immune system that had been fighting on too many fronts for too long.

The athletic mindset — strategic, disciplined, willing to suffer for the goal — is exactly what got me through the complex case. I approached my health the way I approached racing: study the track, build a plan, execute with precision, adjust on the fly, never quit.

The same grit that made me a pro athlete made me a complex case strategist. The arena just changed.
