Livestream Funnels — Pick Your Play
Funnel 1
The Prediction Lock
"I just locked my Hangtown picks. They drop to the Underground list 1 hour before Moto 1."
How it works: During the live stream, tease your race-day picks but don't reveal them. Tell viewers the full breakdown goes to Underground members only before the race. Drop the link in chat.
Best for: Mid-week preview shows & Thursday/Friday streams
Funnel 2
The Gear Giveaway
"We're giving away a Gate Drop tee from MerchJockey right now. Drop your email in the Underground to enter."
How it works: Pull up the Underground signup page on screen. Give viewers 2 minutes to sign up. Pick a winner live from the new signups. Ship them a shirt from MerchJockey. Cost: one shirt per stream.
Best for: Any live stream. Drives instant signups. Use every week.
Funnel 3
The Gift a Fan Challenge
"Someone in chat — tag a moto friend who NEEDS to be watching this. If they sign up for the Underground, you BOTH get entered to win this week's track shirt."
How it works: Viewers tag friends in YouTube chat. Friends click the pinned link and sign up. Both get giveaway entries. Viral loop — one viewer becomes two subscribers.
Best for: Race-night streams when energy is high. Doubles your reach.
Funnel 4
The MerchJockey Flash Sale
"For the next 30 minutes, Underground members have a code for 25% off everything at MerchJockey. Not on the list? Link in the description."
How it works: Announce a time-limited discount code during the stream. Code only goes to Underground members via the welcome email. Creates urgency + FOMO. Non-members sign up to get access.
Best for: Post-race streams. Fans are hyped and impulse-buy.
Funnel 5
The "What I'm Hearing" Tease
"I heard something today about a rider switch that I can't say on stream. It's going in this week's Underground email. Get on the list."
How it works: Tease insider info during the live stream but hold the details for the email. Creates curiosity gap. Viewers sign up because they NEED to know. Deliver in the next email.
Best for: Off-season, silly season, or when rumors are flying
